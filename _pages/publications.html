Black Holes Holography

Expand Details

Investigated the holographic properties of Reduced Kiselev Black Holes under Prof. Tower Wang's supervision. Our main focus was on symmetry analysis, extending the original findings of identical symmetry groups in the near-horizon limit to different black hole. We explored the observational properties of Reduced Kiselev Black Holes within the context of black hole accretion disk dynamics and holographic duality, incorporating Strominger's insights on the holography of the photon ring.

Here, I summarize key aspects of our work: 1. Extending to Kiselev black holes resulted in trivial outcomes. 2. Reviewed the construction of symmetries. 3. Demonstrated that our model does not yield non-trivial results.
